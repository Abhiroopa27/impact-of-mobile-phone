Disadvantages

Health Risks: Excessive use of mobile phones can lead to various health issues such as eye strain, neck and back pain,disrupted sleep patterns, and potential long-term effects such as radiation exposure, posing risks to children's physical well-being.

Addiction: Children can become addicted to their mobile phones, spending excessive amounts of time on screens, which can lead to decreased physical activity, poor academic performance, social withdrawal, and dependency issues.

Cyberbullying and Online Dangers: Mobile phones provide a platform for cyberbullying, harassment, and exposure to inappropriate content, leading to psychological distress, anxiety, depression, and other mental health issues in children.

Privacy Concerns: Children may be unaware of the risks associated with sharing personal information online, leading to privacy breaches, identity theft, and other dangers such as online predators and scams.

Impact on Social and Cognitive Development: Excessive screen time on mobile phones can hinder the development of essential social skills such as face-to-face communication, empathy, and conflict resolution, as well as impair cognitive development, attention span, and academic performance in children.
